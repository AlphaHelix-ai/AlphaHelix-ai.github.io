About me

I’m a microbiology undergraduate student with an insatiable curiosity. While my academic journey revolves around the fascinating world of microorganisms, my heart beats for something entirely different: coding.

HTML and CSS are my playgrounds—I love crafting elegant web pages, meticulously arranging elements, and bringing designs to life. Whether it’s tweaking the layout or fine-tuning the colors, I find solace in the lines of code that shape our digital experiences.

Coding isn’t just a skill; it’s my creative outlet. When I’m not peering through microscopes, I’m immersed in the syntax of tags and selectors. My hobby transcends mere logic; it’s an art form—an expression of my ever-curious mind.

And speaking of curiosity, I’m perpetually in pursuit of knowledge. Whether it’s unraveling the mysteries of cellular biology or mastering a new CSS framework, I thrive on learning. Each challenge is an opportunity to expand my horizons, and every bug I squash is a victory.

So, here’s to the intersection of science and code—a place where curiosity fuels creativity, and learning knows no bounds.
